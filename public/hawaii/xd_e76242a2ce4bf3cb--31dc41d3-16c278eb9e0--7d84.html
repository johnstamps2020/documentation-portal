Pricing for rating

The price of a policy is determined by costs on the policy. Typically, each coverage on a policy has an associated cost. Costs are often premium but can also be taxes. The price of the policy is the sum of the costs. Two types of pricing are:
Basis pricing
Cost is based up another attribute of the policy. For example, the cost of replacement vehicle coverage is a percentage of the vehicle value.
Exposure pricing
Cost is based upon exposure to risk. For example, the cost of stolen vehicle coverage is based upon the garage location.

In APD, you can specify that costs can be overridden, collected separately, or itemized for billing.

XMind

In the mind map, you can add a Pricing marker to a topic, and add a relationship between the Pricing topic and its basis. Pricing in the mind map is informational only; it is not imported to APD.

For example, a Cell Phone risk object has a Loss of Phone coverage with a Phone Premium pricing topic. The basis of Phone Premium is Price when new. Price when new is a money topic under Phone Details.

APD

When you import a mind map to APD, pricing markers and their relationships are dropped. You can use the pricing relationships from the mind map as a guide to adding pricing information to your visualized product in APD.

In a visualized submission, you can define pricing details in the Pricing screen. Define the parameters and rates to be considered during the rating process for the associated lines and risk objects.

For Rating integration, if you have defined the pricing in visualize mode, the CostData object is automatically built as part of code generation. You need to update the rateSlice function.

Costs on Pricing screen

On the Pricing screen in a submission, you can specify coverage costs and other costs for risk objects. For costs, the Code column includes typical costs, such as premium, VAT, and GST. You can also define your own costs.

Add pricing to a risk object

Specify pricing for a risk object in a product line.

Before you begin

Product Design Mode must be set to Designer or above.

About this task

This task uses the following mind map pricing relationship as a example. Cell Phone is a risk object on the Personal Property product line.

Procedure

  1. Start a submission for a visualized product.
  2. On the Risk Details screen, select the Risk Objects tab.
  3. Select Add > Add risk_object. In this example, risk_object is Cell Phone.
  4. Enter values on the Risk Details tab.
  5. Go to the Coverages. In this example, go to the Phone Coverages tab.
  6. Select the coverages that you wish to price. In this example, select Loss of Phone.
  7. When you are done selecting coverages, click OK.
  8. Navigate to the Pricing screen.
    The risk object you just added appears under Risk Objects.
  9. Click the risk object link. Using the example, click Cell Phone.
  10. Your coverage, Loss of Phone, is listed in the Coverage Costs table.
  11. In Cost Type, select Add Cost from the drop-down list.
  12. Enter fields for this cost. For this example, create basis pricing by entering the following information:
    OptionDescription
    Code premium
    Basis Attribute Cell Phone: Price when new
    Rate .014
