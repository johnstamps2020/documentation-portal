Matching up-front payments to billing instructions

Sometimes, a policyowner who is requesting new or additional coverage provides an up-front payment at the time the request is made. When this happens, BillingCenter receives two things:

  • A billing instruction from the PAS for the new or changed policy.
  • A payment confirmation, which could come from the PAS or from a payment gateway or financial institution.

Either the billing instruction and payment confirmation could arrive first. Consequently, the incoming payment is entered into BillingCenter as a suspense payment because the account and policy that the payment applies to is not always known.

After BillingCenter receives the items, it needs to match the items together. BillingCenter has several behaviors to ensure this occurs.

Offer numbers

The BillingInstruction entity and the SuspensePayment entity both have an OfferNumber field. This is a short text field that the PAS and the external payment system use to identify which billing instruction a given suspense payment corresponds to.

Matching items when the billing instruction is received

Whenever BillingCenter receives a billing instruction, if there is a value in the OfferNumber field, BillingCenter checks for suspense payments with the same offer number. If any exist, BillingCenter applies these payments to the policy associated with the billing instruction.

Matching items when the suspense payment is received

Whenever BillingCenter receives a suspense payment, if there is a value in the OfferNumber field, BillingCenter checks for any policy period with the same offer number. If one exists, BillingCenter applies the payment to that policy period.

Matching Items from the suspense payment batch process

There is a small chance that the billing instruction and the suspense payment arrive at the same time. When this occurs, each item could be managed by a different process. Both processes check the database for the other item. But because neither process is complete, neither process is able to see the other item in the database. Consequently, both items get committed to the database without being matched to the other item.

The Suspense Payment batch process checks for suspense payments with offer numbers that match existing policy period offer numbers. Up-front suspense payments eventually gets applied to the correct policy period, even if the two items are received at the same time.

Because of this possibility, Guidewire recommends scheduling the Invoice batch process to run after the Suspense Payment batch process. This helps to ensure that suspense payments are applied correctly before invoices are sent.
