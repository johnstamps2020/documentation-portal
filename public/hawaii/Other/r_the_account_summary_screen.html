The Account Summary screen

The Account Summary screen is designed to optimize the productivity of customer service representatives (CSRs). In the course of providing service to policyholders, CSRs perform a series of common operations. For example, they locate the relevant account, verify the account is in good standing, and answer basic financial questions about the account. They can also navigate to other locations to access more detailed information about a specific area. The Account Summary screen is designed to make it easy for CSRs to performs these common operations. Essential information is presented in an uncluttered and easy-to-interpret format. Navigation markers are clearly indicated to view additional information.

Name

Description

Account Summary toolbar

Close Account button

Close the account. For more information, see Closing accounts.

Start Delinquency button

Start a delinquency for the account. For more information, see Starting delinquencies manually.

Overview section

Name

 The account name.

Account Since

The length of time that account has existed in BillingCenter.

If the account has a service tier (such as Platinum, Gold, or Silver), an icon appears before the length of time designating the account's tier.

Primary Contact

 The name of the primary contact.
Address The postal address of the primary contact.

Phone/Email

The phone number and email of the primary contact.

Delinquencies

The number of delinquencies the account has had in the past 12 months.

If the account is currently delinquency, a delinquency icon (a calendar with an !) appears before the number of delinquencies.

Currency

 The account's currency.

Send Invoices By

The channel used to send invoices to the account (such as Email or Mail).

Payment Instrument

 The account's payment instrument.

Notes section

 This section displays the most recently created account note. If the account has no notes, BillingCenter shows text stating "There are no notes on this account.".

+ button

Add a new note to the account.

Pencil icon

Edit the most recent note.

Most Recent Note

Click the label to view the Account Notes screen, which lets a user search for and view all of the account's notes.

Date

The date of the most recent note. If the account has no notes, this field is not visible.

Topic

The topic of the most recent note. If the account has no notes, this field is not visible.

Subject

 The subject of the most recent note. If the account has no notes, this field is not visible.

Text

 The text of the most recent note. If the account has no notes, this field is not visible.

Active Policies

The number of active policies owned by the account.

Financials section

Current Payment Expected

The account's current outstanding amount. This is the amount that has been billed and not yet paid.

Past Due

The amount on the account that is past due.

If the amount past due is greater than zero, a past due icon (a red clock) appears before the past due amount.

Late Fees

 The total of all late fees that are unpaid. If there are late fees, there is an additional control that links to the Account Late Fee Reversal wizard.

Next Invoice Due

This field is visible for accounts that use account-level billing only.

The amount due of the next invoice to become due. The date the payment is due is included in the field label.

The value is a link you can click to view the Invoices screen.

Last Payment Received

The amount of the last payment received. The date the payment was received is included in the field label.

The value is a link you can click to view the Payments screen.

If no payments have been received, then the label reads No Payment Received.

"Each policy is billed separately"

 This label is visible for accounts that use policy-level billing. If the account uses account-level billing, this label is not visible.

Pie chart

 The pie chart displays the money for the account divided into the following categories:
  • Unbilled
  • Paid
  • Written Off
  • Billed
  • Past Due

Default Unapplied

The amount of money in the account's default unapplied fund.

This field appears only if the account has one unapplied fund. In other words, the field appears if the account:
  • Uses account-level billing.
  • Uses policy-level billing without cash separation.
  • Uses policy-level billing with cash separation, but for some reason has no policies yet.

Total Unapplied

 The total amount of money in the account's default unapplied fund and all of its designated unapplied funds. The value is a link you can click to view the Unapplied Funds Details screen.
This field appears only if the account has two or more unapplied funds. In other words, the field appears if the account:
  • Uses policy-level billing with cash separation and has at least one policy.

Suspense Item Amount

 The total amount of all suspense items for the policy.

Payoff Amount

 The amount that must be paid to fully pay all policies and settle all account-level charges.

Planned Invoices

 The number of planned invoices for the account.

Policy Periods list

Policy Period Status filter

Select the policy periods to display based on their status. In the base configuration, the possible statuses are:

  • All (Open/Canceled/Reinstated)
  • Open
  • Canceled
  • Reinstated

(Product icon)

The first column of the list shows an icon indicating the policy's product, such as a car for Personal Auto or a house for Homeowners. If no product is specified, a policy icon is shown.

(Past Due icon)

The second column of the list shows a past due icon (an alarm clock) if there is an amount past due on the policy period.
(In Force icon)

The third column of the list shows an icon indicating the policy period's in force status. In the base configuration, the possible statuses are:

  • In force (policy with a check mark in the lower right corner)
  • Not in force (policy with a slashed circle in the lower right corner)

Policy #

The policy period's number

Named Insured

The named insured on the policy period.

Effective Date

 The policy period's effective date.

Expiration Date

 The policy period's expiration date.

Cancellation Date

 If the policy has ever been canceled, the date of the most recent cancellation.

Reinstatement Date

 If the policy has been reinstated, the date of the most recent reinstatement.

Total Value

 The total value of the policy.

The screen also includes an alert to warn end users that the account has a policy that is about to go into delinquency. The alert is visible when there is a balance due today that has not yet been received and the account does not have any other active delinquencies.
